# Hi, I'm Adiba Akter 👋

I'm a Computer Science graduate student at Illinois Institute of Technology, focused on software engineering, AI, and building reliable, user-centered systems.

I like working at the intersection of engineering, product quality, and applied AI. My experience includes software development, QA engineering, public-sector web systems, data engineering, accessibility-focused projects, and deep learning research. I enjoy taking ideas from early requirements to working products that are tested, usable, and meaningful.

Currently, I'm a Software QA Engineering Intern at International Farming Investment Management, where I support FarmAdvisor, an agricultural investment management platform. My work focuses on testing client-facing workflows, validating product features, documenting defects, reviewing product behavior, and helping improve release readiness.

I'm also involved in deep learning research at Illinois Tech, where I study model performance and robustness through image classification experiments, seen vs. unseen subtype evaluation, and performance gap analysis. Outside of research and internships, I serve as Corporate Chair for the Society of Women Engineers at Illinois Tech, where I help support professional development, industry engagement, and student opportunities.
